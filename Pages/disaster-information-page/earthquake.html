Earthquake

The Philippines, which is located along the Pacific "Ring of Fire," a significant area of seismic activity, is among the nations most vulnerable to earthquakes worldwide. Active faults, such as the extensive Philippine Fault Zone, which stretches over 1,200 kilometers, intersect the archipelago. PHIVOLCS reports an average of roughly 30 tremors each day due to the country's geological location, which causes frequent earthquakes. [Source: PNA]

Particular dangers are associated with major fault systems. For instance, Metro Manila's densely populated districts and neighboring provinces are traversed by the West Valley Fault, which is a component of the Marikina Valley Fault System. According to research, a "Big One" event (magnitude ~7.2) might result in tens of thousands of fatalities and extensive structural damage, making an earthquake along this fault potentially catastrophic. Because of these dangers, engineers and city planners use the Seismic Hazard Atlas issued by DOST-PHIVOLCS to create more earthquake-resistant structures. [Source: Ambraseys]

There are a number of risks associated with earthquakes that may arise immediately or develop soon after the shaking. These include landslides, particularly in steep or mountainous places; surface rupture, when the ground splits along the fault line; liquefaction, where water-saturated soil acts like liquid and causes structures to tilt or sink; and ground shaking, which can destroy buildings and infrastructure. Coastal towns are at serious risk from tsunamis caused by offshore earthquakes. The depth, location, and kind of ground in the impacted area all affect how serious these risks are.

In the Philippines, earthquakes can have serious consequences. While offshore or trench-related earthquakes may result in tsunamis, shallow earthquakes may induce severe ground shaking, surface rupture, landslides, or even liquefaction, depending on the soil conditions. Tsunami waves and substantial damage were caused by historical occurrences such as the 1990 Bohol Sea earthquake (magnitude 6.8). More recently, in September 2025, an earthquake with a magnitude of 6.9 occurred off the coast of Cebu.

Being ready is essential since it is impossible to predict the precise timing or location of an earthquake. Communities in high-risk areas need to establish evacuation plans, secure heavy furniture, and adhere to building requirements. While the Seismic Hazard Atlas assists engineers and municipal governments in planning safer infrastructure, PHIVOLCS offers real-time earthquake information and resources for public awareness.
