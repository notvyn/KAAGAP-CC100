Before a Disaster

The best strategy to lessen damage and save lives is to prepare before a calamity strikes. The National Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Council (NDRRMC) advises households to start by learning about local hazards, including landslides, typhoons, floods, earthquakes, and volcanic eruptions. Families can create a suitable emergency plan and determine safe areas, escape routes, and meeting places by being aware of local hazards. In order to stay informed, many communities in the Philippines rely on hazard maps and advisories from PHIVOLCS, PAGASA, and the Department of Science and Technology (DOST).

Creating a family emergency plan is another crucial step before a tragedy occurs. This entails delegating tasks to every member of the family, preserving crucial phone numbers, determining two escape routes, and getting ready for potential communication breakdowns. The Philippine Red Cross advises setting up and rehearsing a catastrophe drill at home so that everyone, particularly the elderly and children, is aware of what to do and where to go. To prevent loss or damage during emergencies, it is also advised to store duplicates of critical documents in waterproof containers.

Making a Go Bag or Emergency Kit is equally crucial. The kit should include necessities like bottled water, non-perishable food, flashlight, batteries, first-aid supplies, medications, hygiene products, face masks, whistles, and a portable radio, according to instructions from the Office of Civil Defense (OCD) and Red Cross. Families in high-risk areas should also bring things tailored to their needs, such as durable shoes for earthquake rubble, rope for floods, and dust masks for volcanic ashfall. When warnings are given, homes can safely and swiftly evacuate if supplies are prepared ahead of time.

Lastly, it's critical to stay updated via trustworthy sources. Families are assisted in making prompt decisions by PAGASA weather reports, PHIVOLCS volcano and earthquake advisories, NDRRMC alerts, and LGU announcements. Communities are urged to enroll in early warning systems, heed local government advisories, and take part in barangay-level preparedness initiatives prior to any calamity. Every family may increase their resilience and increase their chances of remaining safe long before a tragedy strikes by combining preparation, practice, and awareness.
