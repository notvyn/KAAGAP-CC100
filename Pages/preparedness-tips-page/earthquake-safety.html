Earthquake Safety

Preparation for earthquakes starts at home. Make sure your house is structurally sound by, if at all possible, having an experienced engineer examine older structures or homes constructed prior to current building regulations. Place heavier items on lower shelves, secure large furniture and appliances by bolting cabinets or bookcases to the walls, and refrain from keeping breakables overhead. Make an emergency kit (go-bag) with the following necessities: cash, a battery-powered radio (or another way to receive alerts), drinking water and non-perishable food (enough for at least three days), a flashlight and extra batteries, a first-aid kit, and critical documents. [Source: Philstar]

The "duck, cover, and hold on" principle should be followed during an earthquake at home, at school, or at work: drop to your hands and knees, seek shelter behind a sturdy table or desk (or against an internal wall if no shelter is available), shield your head and neck, and hold on until the shaking stops. Choose an open space away from buildings, trees, power lines, and steep hills if you're outside. Before reentering buildings following an earthquake, look for structural damage, gas leaks, shattered glass, or debris. You should also be on the lookout for aftershocks, which can happen days or weeks after the original tremor. [Source: RealQuezon]

Being prepared for earthquakes also entails being aware of your surroundings and making advance plans. Decide on a meeting spot with family members or roommates, and identify safe areas and escape routes in your residence, place of employment, or educational institution. The National Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Council (NDRRMC), the Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (PHIVOLCS), and local disaster-management offices may issue warnings, advisories, or instructions. Keep important phone numbers and emergency hotlines close at hand. You and your community will be in a better position to remain safe during earthquakes if you combine knowledge, readiness, and calm action.
