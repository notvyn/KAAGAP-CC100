Flood Safety

Being prepared can save lives and minimize damage when severe rain or flooding is anticipated. Make sure you stay up to date on PAGASA weather information and are aware of whether your location is vulnerable to flooding before floods occur. Prepare an emergency kit (also known as a "go-bag") with necessities such as drinking water, non-perishable food, flashlight and extra batteries, first aid supplies, necessary medications, and a battery-operated radio or alert system. Store important clothing, documents, and valuables in waterproof containers or on higher shelves.

Put safety first if flooding begins or a flood warning is issued. Steer clear of driving or walking through floodwaters; even a few inches of flowing water can be hazardous or conceal dangers like submerged debris or open drains. Turn off the electricity at the main switch, secure utilities (gas, water), and relocate to higher ground or evacuate if instructed by authorities. If you are forced to remain at home, keep your pets and household belongings on higher floors and stay away from toxic floodwater.

Don't go back home until the authorities say it's safe after the floodwaters have subsided. Before turning on utilities, thoroughly inspect your home for structural damage, damaged electrical wiring, gas leaks, or other potential dangers. To reduce health hazards, discard any potentially contaminated food or water and clean and disinfect objects and surfaces that have come into contact with floodwater. Additionally, assist your neighbors, particularly those who are at danger, and keep an eye out for potential secondary hazards like waterborne illnesses.

[Source: Cosmo and GMANetwork]
