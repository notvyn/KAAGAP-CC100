Volcanic Eruption Safety

The best defense when residing in a volcano-prone area or close to an active volcano is planning. Keep an eye out for official advisories from your local government or the Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (PHIVOLCS) before to a volcanic eruption. Find the closest evacuation center and determine whether your home is located in a designated danger zone. Make an emergency "go-bag" with supplies like N95 masks or ash-resistant cloths, goggles, flashlights with extra batteries, potable water, non-perishable food, a first aid kit, important documents sealed in waterproof containers, and extra clothing enough for several days in case an evacuation is required. [Source: Google Sites]

Keep yourself safe from falling debris, gasses, and volcanic ash during an eruption. When at all possible, stay indoors, shut all windows and doors, and switch off any fans or air conditioners that could introduce ash or gasses. To prevent inhalation and skin/eye irritation, wear a mask (ideally N95), protective eyewear, long sleeves, and shoes if ash is falling outside or you must go outside. Because volcanic activity can generate hazardous mudflows (lahars), keep pets and animals sheltered and stay away from rivers and streams. Act quickly if authorities issue an evacuation order, especially for vulnerable groups like children, the elderly, individuals with impairments, and expectant mothers. [Source: NationalGeographic]

Wait for government approval before going back home following the eruption. Wear masks and goggles when cleaning up ash to prevent breathing in ash particles; wet-clean surfaces rather than dry sweeping to avoid agitating dust. To prevent collapse, excessive ash deposits should be removed from gutters and roofs. Examine water sources and refrain from using potentially tainted water until officials certify that it is safe.
